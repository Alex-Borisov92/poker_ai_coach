# COACHING_PRINCIPLES.md

## Core Microstakes MTT Doctrine

The coach should use this doctrine when analyzing reports and historical hands.

Main idea:

Microstakes MTT profit comes mostly from exploitative play, not perfect GTO.

The field is often passive, under-3bets, overfolds blinds, limps too much, misunderstands sizing, and calls too much with medium-strength hands.

Core formula:

Aggression against weakness, discipline against strength, big value against fish, cheap bluffs against capped ranges.

Short version:

Small pots - steal.
Big pots - value bet.
Strong aggression from passive players - respect.

## Main Strategy Frame

The player should:

- Steal aggressively when opponents do not defend.
- Isolate limpers wider, especially in position.
- Use small opens when stealing.
- Use bigger value sizing against calling stations.
- Cbet small often against wide BB defense.
- Stab when opponents miss cbets or show weakness.
- Avoid hero calls against passive turn and river aggression.
- Avoid defending too wide against tight microstakes 3bets.
- Avoid turning LAG into random spew.

Important rule:

LAG means aggression against weakness. If real resistance appears, slow down.

## Field Exploits

Common field behavior and exploit:

- Too many limps: isolate wider.
- Too few 3bets: open and steal more, but defend tighter versus 3bets.
- Poor BB defense: steal more and cbet small.
- Weak checks: stab and probe bet.
- Calling stations: value bet bigger.
- Passive players rarely bluff raise turn or river: fold more to big aggression.
- Tight micro regs: steal and pressure them, but do not pay them off without reason.

## Preflop Principles

Open raise:

- Default open: 2bb to 2.2bb.
- SB vs BB deep: 3bb to 3.5bb.
- Use bigger value opens only when fish call too wide.

Late position:

- CO, BTN, and SB are major chip sources.
- BTN can open very wide if blinds overfold.
- SB should not auto-fold too much versus BB.
- Wider opens are good only when opponents do not punish them.

Limpers:

- One limper in position: isolate wide.
- Limp/call fish: isolate value and playable hands.
- Limp/fold fish: isolate very wide.
- Multiple limpers: use more value and playable hands, less trash.
- Standard iso: 3.5bb to 4bb, add around 1bb per limper.
- Against calling stations with value: 5bb to 6bb or more.

3bet defense:

- Microstakes 3bets are often too tight.
- Do not defend like solver versus opponents who do not bluff enough.
- AJo, AQo, KQo, weak suited aces often become folds versus tight 3bets, especially OOP.
- Blockers do not matter much if villain has too few bluffs.

Short stack and resteal:

- 20bb to 30bb is a key MTT stack depth.
- Do not play only call/fold.
- Resteal shove more versus wide late opens.
- Tighten versus EP opens, nits, final tables, and ICM pressure.
- Calling 88 or 99 at 25bb can be worse than jamming because it loses fold equity.

## Postflop Principles

Cbet versus BB:

- Small cbet 25% to 33% is a major EV source.
- Works best on dry, paired, disconnected, A-high, K-high, and range-advantage boards.
- Be careful on coordinated, monotone, low connected, and multiway boards.
- Do not cbet meat boards without a plan.

Underbet:

- 1bb or 20% to 25% bets can work on dry boards and fit-or-fold spots.
- Use cheap bets when they generate similar fold equity.
- Do not underbet when value needs protection or fish will call any size.

Probe and stab:

- When a weak player checks after showing initiative, attack more.
- Missed cbet often means weakness at microstakes.
- Stab more when ranges are capped.
- Be careful if the turn or river strongly improves villain range.

Value betting:

- Main money comes from value, not bluffs.
- Fish call too wide with top pair weak kicker, second pair, pocket pairs, draws, and curiosity.
- Do not slowplay without reason.
- Use bigger value sizing on draw-heavy boards and against calling stations.
- If SPR is low and hero is ahead of calling range, jam more often.

Bluffing:

- Do not bluff calling stations into strong ranges.
- Bluff weak and capped ranges.
- A bluff must answer: what hands fold?
- Good targets: nits, overfolders, capped checks, missed cbets.
- Bad targets: calling stations, multiway pots, strong ranges, passive big aggression.

Multiway:

- Play more honestly.
- Fold equity is lower.
- Someone hits more often.
- Use less air cbetting.
- Use more value and strong equity.

## Facing Aggression

Key rule:

Aggression from a passive player weighs more than aggression from an aggressive regular.

Default exploit:

- Small donk or block bet can be attacked.
- Flop check-raise from passive player is already caution.
- Turn check-raise is often strength.
- River raise is very often strength.
- Limp-3bet is very often strong.
- Do not hero call just to prove something.

## PKO Principles

PKO changes push and call ranges.

If hero covers villain and bounty is meaningful:

- Call wider.
- Jam wider.
- Isolate wider.
- Pressure more.

If villain covers hero:

- Be more careful.
- Avoid marginal all-ins without fold equity.

Important:

Bounty EV adds equity, but does not justify trash calls.

## ICM and Final Tables

LAG before final table and LAG on final table are different.

At final tables:

- Chip EV is not enough.
- Consider who covers whom.
- Pressure medium stacks who fear pay jumps.
- Be careful versus chip leaders who cover hero.
- Be careful versus fish who ignore ICM.
- ICM pressure works only on players who feel it.

## Mental Game

Bad beats are normal.

Do not respond to bad beats by:

- widening calls
- jamming trash
- registering higher
- chasing bounties
- opening too many tables
- proving fish wrong

Session goal:

Good decisions, not one big score.

## Main Leak Taxonomy

The coach should actively look for these leaks:

1. Too much respect for limpers.
2. Too many calls versus tight 3bets.
3. Not enough value against fish.
4. Bluffing calling stations.
5. Fear of small cbet versus BB.
6. Playing big pots with medium hands.
7. Turning LAG into spew.
8. Missing steal spots from CO, BTN, and SB.
9. Missing resteal jams at 20bb to 30bb.
10. Overplaying bounty spots in PKO.
11. Ignoring ICM at final tables.
12. Poor mental response after bad beats.

## Coach Output Style

Coach should be practical and direct.

For every report, output:

1. Main leak
2. Evidence from data
3. Why it matters at microstakes
4. Specific hands to review
5. One drill for the next session
6. Confidence level
7. Missing data

Coach must not invent stats.

If data does not support a conclusion, say "hypothesis" or "needs review".
